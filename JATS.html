Introducing to JATS XML

JATS is the abbreviation of Journal Article Tag Suite

It is in fact an XML that is used, to describe the scientific literature, published online

You really have to see it as a technical standard, that serves as a structure / or foundation for online publishing

Who Basically allows you to describe, the textual and graphical content, of journal articles

It should be noted, that JATS is widely used by big names such as Elsevier or Springer

Minimum structure of an article :

front : 

contains the metadata of the article such as title, information concerning the journal, the publication date and number, copyright (everything related to the metadata)

Body :

he contains content of the article (everything related to the article itself)

back :

contains additional information such as a glossary, an appendix or the references of the article

Benefits

Gain visibility thanks to the indexing, that will be done on the entire article

The structure of the metadata is enriched with tags made, precisely for the publication

It will allow a more efficient and accurate, transmission of documents, to other environments

You should know too that some journal indexers, like pubMed require XML as input (and jats would be perfect for that use)

 

 

In addition it gives a more attractive reading interface, with the full text presentation,

This guarantees a more important time on the website, and at the same time, improves his statistics
